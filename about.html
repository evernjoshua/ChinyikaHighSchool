About Us

The Head of the school Mr Foster Ruwiza ( In blue suit) receiving the Permanent Secretary for the Ministry of Primary and Secondary Education during the Merit Award Ceremony held on 8 June 2023

Head, Ruziwa

The school has a total population of 1361 students from Form 1 to 6. The A Level school started in May 2019 in response to the high demand for Advanced Level vacancies by the local students. In the previous years, successful O Level students proceeding to A Level would transfer to TC Hardy in Ruwa, Acturus High School in Acturus or relocate elsewhere. A few exceptionally intelligent students would be absorbed by Goromonzi High School if they could afford the fees. Enrollment continues to increase as new people settle in this periurban growth point.

Testimony of Our Achievements 2018 to 2021 Chinyika High School* In three years we have demonstrated without doubt that fees alone cannot sustain the school; that with prudent management of minimal resources, big achievements are possible; that lockdowns did not stop progress and development.

Important Achievements

  • We have built the State of the Art library.
  • We have built a semi detached teachers house.
  • We have built two big fishponds as part of school efforts to establish a secure source of funding for the school and stave off the tendency to raise fees as a source of funding.
  • We have built and completed the laboratory, officially opened in 2020 by the Honourable Member of Parliament Honourable O Bvute on 29 February 2020.
  • The school sank a borehole to increase water supply and prop up garden projects in the school, including fisheries.
  • The school established the best orchard with 480 guava, lemon, mango and peach trees with assistance from the District office's Mrs Ngwenyama.

In three years we have demonstrated without doubt that fees alone cannot sustain the school; that with prudent management of minimal resources, big achievements are possible; that lockdowns did not stop progress and development - most of our achievements were made during lockdown; that Heads should not be cry babies waiting for donations and handouts from government but must redirect energy to establish strategic business units in the school to augment revenue.
